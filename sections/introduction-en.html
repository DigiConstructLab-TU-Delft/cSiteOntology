Introduction back to ToC

Construction sites generate a variety of data from tools such as 3D/4D BIM models, delivery tracking systems, Excel-based weekly look-aheads, and document control platforms. These systems often lack semantic alignment, making integration and automation difficult.

The cSite Ontology was developed as part of a research collaboration between University College London, TU Delft, and the University of Sydney to serve as a semantic integration layer for production control environments. The ontology supports tracking of spatial progress, resource utilisation, activity completion, document workflows, and subcontractor performance.

The ontology was designed through a hybrid process involving real-world case studies, reuse of existing ontologies, and collaborative design workshops with construction stakeholders. It is implemented in OWL and supports SPARQL querying for generating dashboard-ready metrics.
