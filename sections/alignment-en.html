Ontology Alignment back to ToC

The CSite ontology aligns with multiple foundational and domain-specific ontologies to maximize semantic reuse and interoperability across construction datasets and digital twin systems. The imported ontologies provide structure for representing building components, project agents, documents, and activities in a semantically grounded way.

Imported Ontologies

Ontology URL Purpose & Reused Concepts
BOT (Building Topology Ontology) https://w3id.org/bot Models spatial hierarchy of the construction site. Reuses: bot:Site, bot:Building, bot:Storey, bot:Space, bot:Element, bot:containsZone, bot:hasStorey.
BFO (Basic Formal Ontology) https://github.com/BFO-ontology/BFO Provides foundational categories like bfo:Object for modeling physical items like equipment, material batches, and building components.
PROV-O (Provenance Ontology) https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-o/ Used to represent organizational roles and responsibilities in project execution. Reuses: prov:Agent, prov:Organization, prov:Person, prov:wasAssociatedWith, prov:actedOnBehalfOf.
DICE (Digital Construction Entities) https://digitalconstruction.github.io/Entities/ Provides classification for digital documents and submittals. Reuses and extends: dice:InformationContentEntity, dice:WeekPlan, dice:Activity, dice:Plan.
Event Ontology http://purl.org/NET/c4dm/event.owl Used for modeling temporal relations between activities (e.g., event:time, event:hasSubEvent, event:time:before).

These alignments support querying and reasoning across building elements, activities, documents, resources, and spatial zones, and are foundational for integrating CSite with wider Linked Building Data (LBD) ecosystems.
